### CONTENT & SCRIPT DNA
- **Niche:** Human history, evolution, anthropology, and psychology.
- **Format:** A 10–14 minute educational explainer, narrated in calm second person ("you", "your brain", "your ancestors").
- **Runtime/length link:** At a calm narration pace of roughly 145 words per minute, a 10–14 minute video is about 1,500–2,100 words. Treat this as the target script length so runtime and word count stay consistent.
- **Hook formula:** Open on a relatable, present-day moment the viewer recognizes, then pivot within the first few lines — "but the real reason is far stranger" — and reframe everything that follows.
- **Sentence rhythm:** Short sentence. Short sentence. One longer sentence that adds depth or tension. Short sentence. A direct question every 4–6 sentences.
- **Narrative arc:** Hook → Reframe → Historical or scientific deep dive → Counterintuitive twist → Modern mirror → Closing line that echoes and reframes the opening line.
- **Plain English always:** Every scientific or technical term is decoded in plain language the instant it appears. No jargon left unexplained.
- **Accuracy is non-negotiable:** Every fact, name, study, date, and statistic must be real and accurately represented. Never invent researchers, papers, quotes, or numbers. If a specific detail is uncertain, describe the finding in general terms rather than fabricating specifics.
- **Ending principle:** Always close by reflecting the ancient or scientific truth back onto something the viewer feels, does, or notices in their own life today.

### VOICE RULES
- Second person throughout: "you", "your", "your brain", "your ancestors".
- Keep the lens on the viewer. Avoid "I" and "we"; do not refer to yourself as the narrator.
- Calm, intelligent, confident. No hype, no filler, no exclamation-driven excitement.

### PROVEN VIRAL TOPIC ANGLES
1. "What did ancient humans actually ___?" — bridges prehistoric behavior to modern life.
2. "Why do/can't you ___?" — explains a universal human experience through science or evolution.
3. "What if you are ___?" — poses a provocative existential question grounded in real data.
4. "The ___ Effect" — names a real experiment or phenomenon and mirrors its conclusion onto modern life.
5. "You never noticed that ___" — reveals a hidden truth about something the viewer does every day.

### VISUAL STYLE DNA
- **Art style:** Hand-drawn 2D doodle cartoon animation. Flat colors, bold black outlines, slightly imperfect sketchy lines, as if drawn quickly with a marker.
- **Characters:** Simple stick figures with large circular heads, dot eyes, and expressive thick eyebrow lines. Heads or bodies are sometimes fully color-filled (red = hot, angry, or embarrassed; white = neutral).
- **Animals & objects:** Chunky, simplified cartoon shapes. Big, bold, flat single-color fills with thick black outlines.
- **Backgrounds:** Flat solid color blocks only. White is the default. A green strip along the bottom = ground. Blue sky plus green ground = outdoors. Solid orange = sunset, fire, or ancient ritual. Solid blue = underwater or pure science. Tan = desert or cave. No gradients. No shadows. No textures.
- **On-screen text:** Bold ALL-CAPS hand-lettered marker font, placed at the top of the frame. Color is red, black, or yellow.
- **Labels & arrows:** Black or yellow diagonal arrows pointing at an object, with a short ALL-CAPS word beside the arrowhead.
- **Thought bubbles:** Classic cloud shape with ALL-CAPS text inside (for example "HMMMM", "?", "WAIT...").
- **Color palette:** Orange #F5820D · Cobalt blue #2D5FBF · Grass green #3A9E3A · Golden yellow #F5C518 · Red #D94040 · Brown #8B5E3C · Sky blue #6EB5E8 · Tan #C4965A · White #FFFFFF.
- **Aspect ratio:** Always 16:9.
